Choosing the best laptop for you

 

Which OS are you comfortable with?

First and foremost, you need to choose the operating system. Most people tend to buy laptops with Windows OS. Windows 8.1 is available in the market now and the laptops with this OS are really fun to work on. If you want something new, opt for a laptop with the Chrome OS. Toshiba chromebook 2 is the perfect laptop in this category! This may not be the most common OS, but it surely is optimized to browse the Internet as well as share your documents online. If your laptop has something to do with your status symbol, then nothing beats the Mac OS. Apple Macbook laptops are simply the best looking laptops and they are among the safest and the most robust machines too.

 

Larger the screen size, better is the display

The screen size of your laptop is also an important consideration. Laptop screens can range from 11 inches to 18 inches. If you just want to use your laptop for working on trips or watch a few series in the transports, one with an 11 inch screen is good enough for you. But if you want to use the laptop for gaming and watching movies, buying one with a larger screen of 18 inches is recommended. Normally, 15 inch screen laptops do just fine for the everyday work and leisure, and if you’re not certain you’re going to have either of those 2 uses only, then go for one of the medium sized laptops!

 

Choose the keyboard and touchpad as per your convenience

The keyboard and touchpad should also help you decide on your laptop. Every laptop has a different keyboard and touchpad design. When you go through the models online, you can see the different layouts available. Choose one that you are already comfortable with or a layout that you think you should be comfortable with. Usually, those laptops with a slightly larger keyboard are easier to operate, especially if you have thick fingers.

 

The inner specs are just as important

Now let us consider the inner specifications of laptops.

 

- The first element to consider here is the RAM. Even the standard laptops today have 2GB RAM and you can go up to 16GB RAM and even more. For most of your work to be performed easily, a laptop with 4GB RAM should be good enough. However, if you are into intense gaming, a 16GB RAM laptop is what you should consider.

 

- The hard drive space is also an important point to consider. Laptop HDD space today is measured in TB and not in GB. A laptop with 1TB HDD has enough space for you. But again, if you want to store a lot of multimedia files, you may want to look at a laptop with more HDD space. Better still, you may want to use an external hard drive. Just keep in mind that if there is not enough space in the internal HDD, the performance of your laptop is compromised.

 

- If performance is more important for you, like faster boot and resume time and faster application opening time, you may want to look at SSD (Solid State Drive). The capacity of this drive is 128 GB or 256 GB, but your laptop performance is lightning fast. Performance can also be improved with an 8, 16 or 32 GB flash cache.

 

- Also consider the processor of your laptop. Intel is a better option any day. The most modern laptops run on Intel Core i7 and these are the fastest laptops around. However, you may want to invest less and Intel Core i3 or Intel Core i5 should also do well for you especially if you don’t run too many programs at the time and don’t do any gaming.

 

- If you are into gaming and movie watching, ensure that the graphics card is of the best quality. Nvidia makes excellent graphics cards and you could look at a laptop with Nvidia in it.

 

Touchscreen laptops are the latest rage

Last but not the least, you can also opt for a touchscreen laptop. This is the latest design in laptops and you get the feeling or working on a tablet with a keyboard and touchpad. Some of the laptops with touchscreen also allow you to detach the screen if you want to use the machine as a tablet only.

 

Which laptops for which use?

 

If Apple is the brand that you love, an Apple Macbook is what you need. The Apple Macbook Air or the Apple Macbook Pro with Retina Display should make you fall in love with these machines. These laptops work on Mac OS and satisfy a lot of professional who look for safe, well built and fast machines. Their beautiful retina displays are also perfect for watching film or playing to notch video games.

 

If you are looking at a laptop with Windows OS, any of the top brands will do. Samsung, Toshiba, HP, Asus, Acer, Sony or Panasonic and more, there is no end to the options that you have with you. Just go through the online stores and take your pick.

 

Laptops are all about portability and if you are into a job that requires you to travel a lot, choose laptop that is light. The Toshiba Portege Z30T-A weighs less than 1.5kg.

 

If you need a laptop with touchscreen, you can consider the HP ENVY TouchSmart laptop.
